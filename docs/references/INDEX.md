# Reference Index

Master lookup table for all sources used in the EarthworkLLM project (multi-scale geomorphons + a fine-tuned, zero-shot vision-language model for detecting pre-European earthworks from LiDAR in the Lower Mississippi Valley). Search by tags or keywords to find the relevant paper, then check for a summary or claims extraction.

## Format

```
filename | authors_year | tags | key_content
```

## Tag Vocabulary

- `review`: Review articles or meta-analyses
- `methodology`: Papers primarily about methods
- `empirical`: Papers presenting primary data / case studies
- `obia`: Object-based image analysis and morphometric feature detection
- `deep-learning`: CNN / segmentation / detector approaches
- `foundation-model`: Foundation models, VLMs, zero-shot/few-shot, efficient tuning
- `lidar`: LiDAR / DEM-derived terrain analysis
- `geomorphometry`: Landform classification, geomorphons, terrain metrics
- `lmv-context`: Lower Mississippi Valley / Southeastern archaeological context
- `false-positive`: Ancillary-data filtering, precision, modern-feature suppression
- `transfer`: Transfer / cross-domain / zero-shot generalization

## Access status

- `OPEN`: full text obtained in `pdfs/` (open access)
- `PAYWALL`: full text not auto-acquirable; needs institutional access (process from PDF once supplied)

hu_2021.pdf | Hu et al. 2021 | foundation-model, methodology, transfer | OPEN. Introduces LoRA: freezes pretrained weights and trains small injected rank-decomposition matrices (W0+BA), cutting trainable parameters ~10,000x (stored adapter) and training memory ~3x vs full fine-tuning of GPT-3 175B, with on-par quality and no inference latency. Argues weight changes during adaptation have low intrinsic rank (r=1-2 can suffice). Footnote 6 cautions that a downstream task in a different language than pretraining may need full retraining, a useful caveat for our zero-shot transfer claims. Methods basis for Section 4.2. Claims: hu_2021_claims.md. Summary: hu_2021.md.
dettmers_2023.pdf | Dettmers et al. 2023 | foundation-model, methodology, transfer | OPEN. Introduces QLoRA: backpropagates gradients through a frozen 4-bit quantized base (NF4 storage, BFloat16 compute) into LoRA adapters, finetuning a 65B model on one 48GB GPU while preserving 16-bit performance. Three innovations: 4-bit NormalFloat, Double Quantization, Paged Optimizers. Finds data quality matters more than dataset size and that benchmark performance does not transfer automatically across tasks, supporting our data-efficiency and zero-shot arguments. Exact tuning regime used in our pipeline (Section 4.2). Claims: dettmers_2023_claims.md. Summary: dettmers_2023.md.
radford_2021.pdf | Radford et al. 2021 (CLIP) | foundation-model, methodology, transfer | OPEN. Introduces contrastive language-image pretraining on 400M web (image,text) pairs and text-specified zero-shot transfer; matches ResNet-50 ImageNet accuracy zero-shot with none of its 1.28M labels. Defines zero-shot broadly as generalization to unseen datasets/distributions (the sense matching our unseen-region application). Foundational citation for Section 3. Claims: radford_2021_claims.md. Summary: radford_2021.md.

Anchors Section 3 and supports Section 4.2. Claims: kuckreja_2024_claims.md. Summary: kuckreja_2024.md.

Claims: casana_2017_claims.md. Summary: casana_2017.md.
bommasani_2021.pdf | Bommasani et al. 2021 | foundation-model, review, transfer | OPEN. Argues they are enabled by transfer learning and self-supervision (avoiding annotation cost), are inherently multimodal, and give large gains where task-specific data is scarce, our central motivation. Cautions that emergent models need testing and oversight on consequential tasks. Position paper; cite as framing. Anchors Section 3 and Sections 7.4/Limitations. Claims: bommasani_2021_claims.md. Summary: bommasani_2021.md.
